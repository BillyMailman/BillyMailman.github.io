Downtime Rules

General

Because the Saltmarsh modules aren't a single consecutive campaign, we'll be using Downtime. See PHB Chapter 8, "Adventuring", the last section entitled "Between Adventures".

This also means that your character's lifestyle will need to be chosen, and may matter somehow, details TBD. When we finish a module, I'll say something like "Alright, everyone level to X. It'll be Y days until the next adventure.", which means you need to spend enough gold for your lifestyle for Y days, but you'll also gain Y days to spend on downtime activities.

Some activities, however, will eliminate the need to spend money on your lifestyle. Also, some class or background features may do the same.

All the Downtime Activities from the PHB, DMG, and XGtE are available, with the following exceptions:

  • Building a Stronghold (DMG) - Unsuitable to a campaign on the seas.
  • Carousing (DMG) - This activity plays out more like gambling. XGtE has another Carousing activity, and also has actual Gambling, so this is redundant.
  • Most forms of crafting, buying, and/or selling Magic Items (DMG/XGtE) - GoS has a vendor. Healing potions are still craftable, and spell scrolls may be scribed
  • Gaining Renown (DMG) - I don't plan to use the DMG's Guild/Organisation Renown mechanic.
  • Training to Gain Levels (DMG) - We'll be using Milestone Levelling, based on the main GoS modules. No need to train.
  • All the complications and Rivals stuff from XGtE - If you guys all buy into the idea, I can maybe use this, but the biggest problem I see is that it only works out-of-the-box with the XGtE Downtime Activities.

Downtime Activities

This is a list of downtime activities from official WotC books that you can do.

If you want to do something, and it's not listed below, just ask me about it. For simple things, like "I want to scribe all the spells from this spellbook we acquired into my book.", or other class/race/etc. features that take time, we'll resolve it out with those features' rules. For anything else, give me a day or two, and I'll do what I can to come up with reasonable rules.

Buying/Selling/Crafting Items

Buying Basic Equipment

Not actually a downtime thing, but I'm listing it here.

Rules:

  • There are shops, merchants, and the like in Saltmarsh, from which you can buy most non-magical items.

Crafting (PHB)

Allows you to craft non-magical objects (Note: Potions of Healing are also allowed).

Rules:

  • Requires proficiency with relevant tools.
  • Requires the relevant tools.
  • May require access to other equipment (forge for smithing, etc.)
  • Must spend one day per 5gp of the item's value.
  • Must spend half the item's value in materials.
  • Multiple characters with the relevant proficiency may work together, each spending time and money, and pooling it together.
  • While crafting, you can maintain a modest lifestyle without having to pay 1 gp per day, or a comfortable lifestyle at half the normal cost.

Scribing a Spell Scroll (XGtE)

Allows you to create spell scrolls

Rules:

  • Must have proficiency in Arcana.
  • Must have the spell known/prepared/etc., as appropriate to your spellcasting class.
  • Must have any material components during scribing. If the components are consumed by casting, they are consumed in scribing
  • Costs depend on spell level:
    • Cantrip: 1 day, 15gp
    • Level 1: 1 day, 25gp
    • Level 2: 3 days, 250gp
    • Level 3: 5 days, 500gp
    • Level 4: 10 days, 2,500gp
    • Level 5: 20 days, 5,000gp
    • Level 6: 40 days, 15,000gp
    • Level 7: 80 days, 25,000gp
    • Level 8: 160 days, 50,000gp
    • Level 9: 240 days, 250,000gp

Employment

Practicing a Profession (PHB)

Allows you to maintain a modest lifestyle for free.

Rules:

  • Must have a relevant profession OR membership in an organisation in-town you can do odd jobs for (temple, guild, etc.)
  • If you use Performance proficiency, you can support a Wealthy lifestyle instead.

Running a Business

Allows you to run a business that you own, to make money.

Rules:

  • Must own a business
  • Spend as many days as you want, more spent at once (up to 30) is better.
  • Roll D%, and add the number of days (max +30). Consult a table in DMG to see how well the business does.
  • Bigger businesses make more money, but cost more to maintain if you're not helping to run them.

Working (XGtE)

Allows you to maintain a lifestyle for free.

See also Practicing a Profession from PHB, for an option that is less variable.

Rules:

  • Make a check. Either Str(Athletics) or Dex(Acrobatics) for simple labour, Int(Tool Proficiency) for skilled labour (not Thieves' Tools, though), or Cha(Performance) for work as an entertainer.
  • Your lifestyle depends on the result:
    • 1-9, Poor lifestyle
    • 10-14, Modest lifestyle
    • 15-20, Comfortable lifestyle
    • 21+, Comfortable lifestyle, and 25gp profit

Gambling (XGtE)

Allows you to gamble your money.

Rules:

  • Costs five days and a stake of 10-1000gp
  • Make a series of three checks:
    • Wis(Insight)
    • Cha(Deception)
    • Cha(Intimidation)
    • Any one of the above skills may be replaced with a gaming set you are proficient in and possess.
  • The check DCs are separate and unknown; the DM will roll for them (2d10+5 for each). This is gambling, after all.
  • The results depend on the number of passed checks:
    • Zero successes, you lost your money, and also go in debt by that amount.
    • One success, you lose half your bet.
    • Two successes, you win back your bet, plus one and a half times its value.
    • Three successes, you win back your bet, plus double.

Pit Fighting (XGtE)

Allows you to participate in pit fights for money. This "includes boxing, wrestling, and other nonlethal forms of combat in an organized setting with predetermined matches."

Rules:

  • Costs five days.
  • Make a series of three checks:
    • Str(Athletics)
    • Dex(Acrobatics)
    • A special Constitution check, no proficiency, but with your Hit Die rolled and added. (I.E., 1d20 + 1dX + Con, where X is your hit die type)
    • Any one of the above checks may be replaced with an attack roll of one of your weapons.
  • The check DCs are separate and unknown; the DM will roll for them (2d10+5 for each). This represents you not knowing what the competition will be, week to week.
  • The results depend on the number of passed checks:
    • Zero successes, you lose your fights, and win nothing.
    • One success, you win 50gp.
    • Two successes, you win 100gp.
    • Three successes, you win 200gp.

Crime (XGtE)

Allows you to rob places for money.

Note that the target names have been changed to fit Saltmarsh better, but the mechanics remain mostly unchanged from XGtE.

Rules:

  • Spend five days and 25gp gathering intel
  • Make three checks, all at the same DC:
    • Dex(Stealth)
    • Dex(Thieves' Tools)
    • Choice of Int(Investigation), Wis(Perception), or Cha(Deception)
  • Four types of target are available:
    • Struggling Merchant. DC10 checks, 50gp reward.
    • Prosperous Merchant. DC15 checks, 100gp reward.
    • Wealthy Citizen. DC20 checks, 200gp reward.
    • Wealthy Named NPC. DC25 checks, 1000gp reward
  • The results depend on the number of passed checks:
    • Zero successes, you are caught and jailed.
    • One success, you get nothing, but escape successfully.
    • Two successes, you get half the listed payout.
    • Three successes, you get the listed payout.

    • For the last tier of target, I'm adding the following rules:

    • You need to target a specific, wealthy, named NPC in town.
    • Getting caught will have additional consequences beyond the normal level of general distrust that people would have towards a convicted criminal.

Training and Preparation

Training (PHB)

Allows you to gain new Tool proficiencies or languages

Rules:

  • You must find an instructor who can teach you.
  • The training lasts for 50 days and costs 250gp. (Note: PHB says 250 days. Screw that.)
  • Intelligence modifier above 0 reduces the training by 5 days and 25gp for each point. No penalty for negatives.

Performing Sacred Rites (DMG)

Allows a pious character to seek the blessings of their god

Rules:

  • Must be pious, and able to serve at a temple affiliated with your god.
  • Costs ten days.
  • Grants Inspiration at dawn each day for the next ten days.

Research (XGtE)

Allows you to learn about a monster, location, item, person, or other noun.

Rules:

  • Must have access to a library or similar source of information
  • Costs five days, and 50gp.
  • Make an Intelligence check. No skill, since this represents information you don't know through your own History/Religion/Arcana/Etc. skills.
  • You may spend additional money on things like access to rarer books, contacting and bribing other researchers, etc. Each 100gp gives +1 to the check, to a maximum of +6.
  • Results:
    • 1-5, you learn nothing.
    • 6-10, you learn one piece of lore.
    • 11-20, you learn two pieces of lore.
    • 21+, you learn three pieces of lore.
  • Each piece of lore is the equivalent of one true statement about a person, place, or thing. Examples include knowledge of a creature’s resistances, the password needed to enter a sealed dungeon level, the spells commonly prepared by an order of wizards, and so on.

Social

Sowing Rumours

Allows you to sow rumours in Saltmarsh

Rules:

  • Must spend 1 gp per day to cover the cost of drinks, social appearances, and the like.
  • Must make a DC15 Cha(Deception) or Cha(Persuasion) check at the end.
  • If the check fails, that rumour won't take hold, even on further attempts.
  • If the check succeeds, "the community’s prevailing attitude toward the subject shifts one step toward friendly or hostile, as the character wishes."

Carousing (XGtE)

Allows you to make contacts.

Rules:

  • Must spend five days and spend gold per day:
    • 10gp at a lower-class bar
    • 50gp at a middle-class bar
    • 250gp at an upper-class bar
  • By default, only upper-class characters can use the upper-class option.
  • After the week ends, make a Cha(Persuasion) check, DM will consult a table, and you will make contacts who may help you in the future
  • A contact gained in this way provides help once, not help for life.

Religious Service (XGtE)

Allows you to earn favours from the clergy of a temple.

Rules:

  • Costs five days of downtime.
  • Must be at a temple "whose beliefs and ethos align with the character’s".
  • At the end, make an Int(Religion) or Cha(Persuasion) check:
    • 1-10, no effect
    • 11-20, you earn one favour
    • 21+, you earn two favours
  • A favour may be asking for political/social help, may be asking for cleric spellcasting at reduced price.
  • A pious character who worships that temple's god may instead spend the favour by praying for an omen, vision, or minor miracle, at DM's discretion.
  • May only store up total favours of 1+Charisma Modifier (minimum 1).
  • If you earn favour(s) and would go over your limit, you can spend extra immediately.

Recovery

These two are somewhat redundant with each other, but work slightly differently, so I'm leaving both here.

Recuperating (PHB)

Allows you to heal long-term ongoing effects.

Rules:

  • Costs three days of downtime.
  • Make a DC15 Con Save. If you do, you may either:
    • End one effect on you that prevents you from regaining hit points.
    • For the next 24 hours, gain advantage on saving throws against one disease or poison currently affecting you.

Relaxation (XGtE)

Allows you to heal long-term ongoing effects.

Rules:

  • Costs one week
  • Must be maintaining at least Modest lifestyle during that week
  • Gain advantage on saving throws to recover from long-acting diseases and poisons during the week.
  • At the end of the week, choose one of the following (not caused by a spell/magical effect with a duration):
    • End one effect that keeps the character from regaining hit points.
    • Restore one ability score that has been reduced to less than its normal value.
